Quick reference — call scripts
Gatekeeper — routing attempt
"Hi, I'm calling for [Owner]. Could you route me to his office?"
If asked what it's regarding: "It's regarding the lead flow on the website." — then stop talking.
Day 1 — Missed calls
"[Owner], I'm Dylan. I'll be quick — I noticed your office line doesn't have a backup for when you're on another call or after hours. For a roofing company, that's usually costing you contracts you don't even know you lost. That's why I'm calling — would a quick demo make sense, even just 10 minutes?"
Day 1 — Low reviews
"[Owner], I'm Dylan. I pulled up your reviews and your count is lower than a few competitors in the area. Homeowners check that before they call — if you're not showing up strong, they're skipping you before you ever get a shot. Would it be totally crazy to do a 10-minute chat to see if this is a fit?"
Day 1 — Website glitch
"[Owner], I'm Dylan. I was on your site and ran into some issues that would make it hard for a customer to actually contact you. You're burning your marketing budget when that happens — that's why I'm calling. Would you be open to a quick demo of a cleaner setup?"
Day 3 — Force clarity
"[Owner], I'm Dylan — I called earlier this week about your lead flow. Just following up to see if it's worth a quick two-minute conversation, or if I should close the loop here."
Day 5 — Decision point
"[Owner], I've reached out a couple times about your lead flow. Before I move on, I want to give you the opportunity for a clear yes or no. No hard feelings either way."
Day 7 — Close the loop
"[Owner], I'm Dylan. I've reached out a few times about protecting your lead flow. I'm going to close the loop on my end — no hard feelings. If the timing ever changes, you know where to find me. Take care."
No answer voicemail — Day 1
"Hey [Owner], this is Dylan — I'll keep it short. I was looking at your setup and noticed something around your lead flow that's worth a two-minute conversation. I'll try you again in a couple days. Talk soon."
